Ecological Impact

Restoring aquatic ecosystem health and biodiversity

Reduction of Microplastic Concentration

Field trials in aquaculture facilities demonstrate 96% reduction in microplastic particle counts (10-1000μm range) within treated water volumes. Continuous operation over 90 days maintained particle concentrations below 50 particles/L, compared to 1,200+ particles/L in untreated control systems.

1,200+ → 50 particles/L 90-day sustained performance

Healthier Aquaculture Outputs

Fish and shellfish raised in AquaPure-treated systems show 89% lower microplastic tissue accumulation, improved growth rates (12% faster time-to-market), and enhanced meat quality scores. Reduced stress indicators and disease incidence translate to healthier, more valuable harvests.

89% lower bioaccumulation 12% faster growth

Cleaner Nearshore Ecosystems

Deployment in coastal water intake systems protects sensitive marine habitats by preventing microplastic infiltration. Monitoring data shows improved water clarity, reduced turbidity (NTU decreased by 42%), and restoration of filter-feeding organism populations in treated zones.

42% turbidity reduction Habitat restoration support

Preventing Plastic-Induced Bioaccumulation

By removing microplastics at the source, our technology breaks the bioaccumulation cycle that magnifies toxin concentrations through food chains. Laboratory analysis confirms 94% reduction in persistent organic pollutants (POPs) and heavy metals in treated system organisms.

94% POPs reduction Food chain protection

Microplastics in seafood pose direct human health risks, with average global consumption estimated at 5 grams per week—equivalent to eating a credit card monthly. By removing microplastics at the source, we protect public health, reduce healthcare burdens, and ensure food safety for billions of seafood consumers worldwide.
