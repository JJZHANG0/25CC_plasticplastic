Understanding microplastic contamination in aquatic environments

Microplastics in Nearshore & Aquaculture Waters

Particles ranging from 10-1000 micrometers infiltrate aquaculture systems through water intake, feed contamination, and environmental runoff. Traditional mesh filters cannot capture particles below 100μm, allowing massive quantities to remain in circulation.

10-1000μm Particle Size Range <100μm Unfiltered by Traditional Systems

Health & Ecological Risks

Microplastics absorb persistent organic pollutants (POPs) and heavy metals, acting as toxic transport vectors. Bioaccumulation through the food chain leads to inflammatory responses, endocrine disruption, and reproductive damage in marine organisms and humans.

Limitations of Existing Prevention Methods

Current approaches rely on passive filtration (membrane, sand, activated carbon) which clog rapidly, require high pressure differential, consume excessive energy (800-2000W), and fail to address submicron particles. Chemical coagulation introduces secondary pollutants and raises operational costs.

Ultrasonic Pretreatment Technology

High-frequency acoustic waves (20-100 kHz) generate controlled cavitation bubbles that create micro-turbulence fields around suspended microplastic particles.

  • Acoustic Radiation Force: Particles experience secondary Bjerknes forces, driving them toward cavitation bubble surfaces
  • Agglomeration Enhancement: Particle collision frequency increases by 400-600%, forming larger aggregate clusters (100-500μm)
  • Surface Modification: Ultrasonic treatment alters particle surface hydrophobicity, improving separation selectivity
  • Energy Efficiency: 150W ultrasonic generator operates for 10-minute intervals per cycle
02

Hydrocyclone Isovortex Separation

Precision-engineered conical hydrocyclone creates stable isovortex flow patterns, exploiting density and size differences between agglomerated microplastics and water.

  • Tangential Inlet Design: Water enters tangentially, generating high-velocity rotational flow (3-5 m/s)
  • Centrifugal Classification: Denser water spirals downward (underflow), lighter plastic aggregates concentrate in vortex core (overflow)
  • Optimal Cone Angle: 20° cone geometry maximizes residence time and separation efficiency
  • Pressure Drop Management: Low-pressure differential (0.3-0.5 bar) minimizes energy consumption
03

Intelligent Automated Control System

Real-time monitoring and adaptive optimization ensure consistent performance across varying water conditions and contamination levels.

  • Turbidity Sensors: Continuous measurement of inlet/outlet water clarity (NTU)
  • Particle Counters: Optical detection of particle size distribution and concentration
  • Adaptive Algorithms: Machine learning adjusts ultrasonic frequency, power, and cyclone flow rate based on real-time data
  • Predictive Maintenance: IoT connectivity enables remote diagnostics and performance optimization
04

Efficient Agglomeration + Precise Removal

The synergistic combination of ultrasonic pretreatment and hydrocyclone separation achieves unparalleled microplastic removal performance.

  • Continuous Flow Design: 3-4 m³ water processed per 30-minute cycle enables high-volume treatment
